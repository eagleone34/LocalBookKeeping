## Brand Identity

- **Product name:** LocalBooks
- **Tagline:** "Your Books. Your Computer. No Subscriptions."
- **What it does:** Desktop bookkeeping software that runs entirely on your computer. Import bank statements, auto-categorize transactions, track income and expenses, and generate financial reports — all without an internet connection or monthly fee.
- **Core problem it solves:** People paying $30+/month for cloud bookkeeping software they barely use, while handing their financial data to third parties. LocalBooks gives them a one-time-purchase alternative that keeps data local and private.
- **Who it's for:** Individuals tracking personal finances, freelancers, side hustlers, home-based business owners, and small businesses with straightforward bookkeeping needs. Not enterprise. Not complex multi-entity accounting.
- **Key differentiators:**
  - **100% local** — data never leaves the user's computer. No cloud sync, no tracking, no account required.
  - **One-time payment** — $49.99 USD, lifetime access, all future updates included. No tiers, no add-ons.
  - **Smart categorization** — learns from user corrections. After the first bank statement, most recurring transactions auto-categorize.
  - **PDF bank statement import** — drag-and-drop PDF or CSV. No bank API connections, so users never share login credentials.
  - **Single-file installer** — download one `.exe`, install in under a minute, start bookkeeping immediately.

- Guaranteeing accuracy of financial calculations or bank statement parsing

- Negative attacks on specific competitors by name — position on our strengths, not their weaknesses
- Promising Mac support with a specific date (it's on the roadmap, no ETA)

- LocalBooks is **beta software**. Do not claim it is production-grade, certified, or guaranteed bug-free.
- It is **not a substitute** for professional accounting, tax preparation, or audited financial statements. If referencing tax use, always frame as "helps you organize for tax time" not "does your taxes."
- **No warranty** on accuracy of calculations or bank statement parsing. Avoid language that implies guaranteed correctness.
- Total liability is capped at the purchase price ($49.99). Do not make claims that exceed this.
- Financial data security depends on the user's own device. We don't store or transmit data, but we don't control their machine either.

## Competitive Landscape

> **IMPORTANT FOR CONTENT GENERATION:** The competitors below are listed for internal context only. **Never name competitors in social media posts.** Position LocalBooks on its own strengths. Use phrases like "subscription bookkeeping tools," "cloud-based alternatives," or "the software I was paying $30/month for" instead of brand names.

### Competitors and positioning

| Competitor | What they are | Our angle against them |
|---|---|---|
| **QuickBooks Online** | Market leader, $30+/month, cloud-based, feature-heavy | Overkill and expensive for simple bookkeeping. $1,080+ over 3 years for features most small users never touch. Position: "You don't need to pay $30/month to categorize your spending." |
| **Wave** | Was free, cloud-based, acquired by H&R Block. Showed ads, shared data with third parties for revenue. | Users lost trust after the acquisition. Position: "Same simplicity you liked, but on your computer — no cloud, no ads, no uncertainty about where your data goes." |
| **FreshBooks** | Cloud-based, $17+/month, focused on invoicing and freelancers | Invoicing-heavy — unnecessary if you just need expense tracking and reports. Position: "If you don't need invoicing, you don't need to pay for it." |
| **Xero** | Cloud-based, $15+/month, popular internationally | Similar to QuickBooks positioning — subscription cost and cloud dependency. |
| **Spreadsheets (Excel/Google Sheets)** | Free or cheap, fully manual | Works but tedious, error-prone, no automation. Position: "Way more organized than a spreadsheet. Way cheaper than QuickBooks." |

### How to position without direct comparison

- Lead with the problem ("tracking finances shouldn't cost $30/month"), not the competitor
- Use cost math: "$49.99 once vs $360/year" — let readers do the comparison themselves
- Emphasize what we do differently (local, private, learns your categories) rather than what they do wrong
- When referencing migration: "I switched from my old bookkeeping app" or "after my previous tool changed direction" — no names needed
- The strongest positioning is a first-person story: "Here's what I use and why" beats "here's why X is bad"
